## Results used

Theorem 2.1 decides whether a given nonsingular integer `2 × 2` matrix belongs to
the semigroup generated by any finite family of nonsingular integer `2 × 2` matrices.
The proof separates determinant-growing factors from unimodular factors: determinant
growth bounds the number of the former in a product for a fixed target, while automata over
`GL(2,ℤ)` handle the intervening factors. Theorem 2.5 decides a more general alternating
matrix equation with regular `GL(2,ℤ)` subsets.

For mortality this is a boundary marker, not a solution. After reducing a minimal mortal
word to rank-one endpoints, the interior question asks whether a nonsingular semigroup
intersects an infinite scalar-zero variety, not whether it contains one fixed matrix. The
fixed-target determinant bound therefore disappears. A promising dimension-two program is
to extend the paper's automata construction from singleton membership to that projective
incidence target.

## Audit notes

The stored PDF is the authors' open 17-page version of the peer-reviewed SODA paper.
Its introduction also records a structural barrier to importing the usual PCP proof into
dimension two: independent pairs of words do not admit the required faithful `2 × 2`
complex-matrix representation. The cited primary source for that barrier should be inspected
before the statement is used as a theorem in our work.
