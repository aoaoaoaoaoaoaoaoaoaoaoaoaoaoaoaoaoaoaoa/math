## Historical relevance

Theorem 3 claims undecidability of four-pair PCP; Corollary 2 consequently claims mortality
undecidable for five `3 × 3` matrices and two `15 × 15` matrices. The refereed 2015 successor
instead proves five-pair PCP, explicitly leaves the four-pair case open, and derives six
`3 × 3` matrices. Neary's own bibliography lists the 2015 paper under “Publications” and this
item under “Technical reports.”

## Audit notes

Treat this as the first public `M₃(5)` proof claim, but not as an accepted establishment of the
theorem. ArXiv still serves only v1 and carries no formal withdrawal or erratum. No published
source diagnosing the exact defect, and no independently verified counterexample to the
four-pair construction, was found in the present search.

Theorem 3's converse concludes with an assertion that every tile choice departing from the
intended simulation must mismatch, without an exhaustive arbitrary-word analysis. The later
publication history and open-problem tables show that this assertion was not accepted, but do
not by themselves constitute a formal disproof. Use “superseded,” “unretained,” or
“previously unestablished”; do not use “retracted,” “withdrawn,” or simply “invalid.” The local
copy is retained because every priority statement must distinguish the present route from this
earlier numerical claim.
