## Results used

Theorem 1 extends the decidable `2 × 2` cases to every finite rational family containing at
most one nonsingular matrix. Theorem 5 states that, for a fixed number of nonsingular
generators, decidability is independent of the number of singular generators. The proof uses
the fact that a minimal mortal product has rank-one endpoints and only nonsingular interior
factors.

## Audit notes

Only arXiv v1 and no peer-reviewed publication were located. Treat it as a useful structural
reduction, not as an unquestioned black box. For the frontier campaign it isolates the first
genuinely unknown two-dimensional core: products between rank-one boundaries drawn from a
semigroup with at least two nonsingular generators.
