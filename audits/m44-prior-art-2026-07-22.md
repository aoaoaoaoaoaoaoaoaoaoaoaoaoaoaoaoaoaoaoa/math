# Prior-art investigation: three common-column \(4\times4\) scalar-zero generators, \(M_4(4)\), paired-role compression, and two obstruction theorems

**Search cutoff:** 22 July 2026  
**Scope:** Publicly indexed papers, preprints and version histories, conference proceedings, journal articles, technical reports, theses, books/chapters, surveys, author copies, institutional repositories, and backward/forward citation chains.  
**Nature of this pass:** Bibliographic only. The proposed arguments were treated as claims to be matched against the literature; they were not repaired, generalized, improved, or independently proved.

## 1. Bottom line

No prior source was found that proves either of the two principal numerical statements under the requested conventions:

1. undecidability of scalar zero reachability for **at most three \(4\times4\) integer matrices**, with every generator sharing first column \(e_1\), and with a **nonempty** control word; or
2. undecidability of mortality for **at most four \(4\times4\) integer matrices**, with the zero matrix required as a **nonempty** product.

In the dimension-first notation used in the request, the verdicts are therefore:

- **structured \(Z_4(3)\): no prior art found**;
- **\(M_4(4)\): no prior art found**.

The nearest established numerical result is Cassaigne–Halava–Harju–Nicolas (CHHN), which proves \(Z_5(3)\) and \(M_5(4)\), not \(Z_4(3)\) or \(M_4(4)\). CHHN also supplies the closest structural precedents: common-column PCP matrices, cyclic phase/block encodings, an invariant-space compression, and a rank-one reduction from scalar zero to mortality. Its structured generator trade saves one state from the literal \(6\times6\) packing but stops at dimension five.

The proposed **rank-one separator principle** is not new in general. Endpoint selection by a rank-one idempotent appears in Halava–Harju–Hirvensalo’s Claus-instance construction, and CHHN gives the general scalar-zero-to-mortality outer-product reduction. What was not found is the proposed converse argument that replaces the usual nonsingularity requirement by a common fixed column.

The general **Hankel-rank lower-bound principle** is classical: over a field, the minimum dimension of an exact linear or weighted-automaton realization equals the rank of the series’ Hankel matrix. Thus a nonzero \(6\times6\) Hankel minor is an established form of certificate excluding every exact five-state realization. No source was found that constructs such a certificate for the scalar coefficient series of the standard CHHN \(6\times6\) packing, much less uniformly for every placement of the rank-one separator.

No source was found for the proposed **phase-fusion forcing theorem**—the assertion that one-dimensional overlap or rank-one punctuation must be confined to a one-sided word plane. The endpoint of that argument is classical only conditionally: once a zero condition is expressed as a fixed-base affine/Presburger relation on positional values, its digit language is effectively regular by the arithmetic-automata literature.

These are bibliographic “no prior art found” conclusions, not claims about unpublished manuscripts, private communications, or material absent from searchable catalogues.

## 2. Conventions used in the audit

The literature reverses the two parameters often enough to create false matches. This report uses the request’s **dimension-first** notation:

\[
M_d(k):\quad
X\subseteq \mathbb Z^{d\times d},\quad |X|\le k,\quad
\exists w\in X^+\ \text{with}\ \mu(w)=0_{d\times d}.
\]

Here \(X^+\) means a **nonempty** product. The empty product is excluded.

For scalar zero reachability:

\[
Z_d(k):\quad
X\subseteq \mathbb Z^{d\times d},\quad |X|\le k,\quad
\exists w\in X^+\ \text{with}\ L\mu(w)C=0,
\]

for specified integer/rational boundary vectors \(L,C\). A zero-in-the-left-upper-corner formulation is the special case \(L=e_1^\top\), \(C=e_1\), or an equivalent fixed-basis formulation. The proposed structured result adds the restriction that every generator has first column \(e_1\).

CHHN writes \(M(k,d\times d)\) and \(Z(k,d\times d)\), with the **generator count first**. Consequently:

- the proposed \(M_4(4)\) is CHHN’s \(M(4,4\times4)\);
- CHHN’s established \(M(4,5\times5)\) is \(M_5(4)\), one dimension larger;
- the proposed \(Z_4(3)\) is CHHN’s \(Z(3,4\times4)\);
- CHHN’s established \(Z(3,5\times5)\) is \(Z_5(3)\), one dimension larger.

For PCP, ordinary PCP requires a nonempty source word. Nicolas’s standard GPCP definition permits a witness in the free monoid and therefore can include the empty witness unless a \(+\) superscript or another nonemptiness condition is imposed. This distinction was checked wherever a bounded GPCP statement was used.

## 4. Exact numerical priority

### 4.1 Paterson and the unbounded-generator baseline

Michael S. Paterson, “Unsolvability in \(3\times3\) Matrices,” *Studies in Applied Mathematics* 49(1):105–107 (1970), proves undecidability of mortality for a finite set of \(3\times3\) **integer** matrices. The number of generators is not bounded by the small fixed constants at issue here. Products are finite and nonempty, and the target is the full zero matrix.

Stable link: https://doi.org/10.1002/sapm1970491105

**Verdict:** foundational related prior art; not numerical prior art for \(M_4(4)\).

### 4.2 Claus-type endpoint constructions

Volker Claus’s bounded PCP and probabilistic-automata constructions introduced special instances with distinguished endpoint behavior. The most relevant later exploitation is Halava–Harju–Hirvensalo (HHH), which uses Claus instances to improve several matrix bounds.

- Volker Claus, “Some Remarks on PCP(\(k\)) and Related Problems,” *Bulletin of the EATCS* 12:54–61 (1980).
- Volker Claus, “The \((n,k)\)-Bounded Emptiness-Problem for Probabilistic Acceptors and Related Problems,” *Acta Informatica* 16:139–160 (1981).

Stable link for the 1981 paper: https://doi.org/10.1007/BF00261257

These sources are historically important for forced first/last roles, but they do not give the proposed \(4\times4\) count or the paired-role quotient.

### 4.3 Halava–Harju–Hirvensalo 2006/2007

Vesa Halava, Tero Harju, and Mika Hirvensalo, “Undecidability Bounds for Integer Matrices Using Claus Instances,” appeared first as TUCS Technical Report 766 (2006) and then in the peer-reviewed *International Journal of Foundations of Computer Science* 18(5):931–948 (2007).

Stable links:

- https://doi.org/10.1142/S0129054107005066
- https://dblp.org/rec/journals/ijfcs/HalavaHH07

Relevant results include scalar/corner reachability bounds for \(3\times3\) integer matrices and a mortality construction using a rank-one idempotent \(B=E_{11}\). Their Theorem 10 absorbs endpoint matrices into products of the form \(BM_1\) and \(M_7B\), and arbitrary products containing \(B\) break into scalar factors. The construction gives seven \(3\times3\) mortality generators, not four \(4\times4\) generators.

**Verdict:** exact prior art for endpoint absorption and rank-one scalar selection; related, not exact, for the proposed theorem.

### 4.4 CHHN 2014: the nearest numerical and structural source

Julien Cassaigne, Vesa Halava, Tero Harju, and François Nicolas, “Tighter Undecidability Bounds for Matrix Mortality, Zero-in-the-Corner Problems, and More,” arXiv:1404.0644.

Version history:

- v1: 2 April 2014;
- v2: 19 April 2014;
- v3: 5 September 2014.

Stable links:

- https://arxiv.org/abs/1404.0644
- https://doi.org/10.48550/arXiv.1404.0644
- https://dblp.org/rec/journals/corr/CassaigneHHN14

Status: arXiv/CoRR preprint, not located as a refereed journal or proceedings publication. Its bounds are nevertheless repeatedly used as the standard unrestricted integer-mortality frontier in later refereed work.

CHHN proves, in dimension-first notation:

\[
M_3(6),\quad M_5(4),\quad M_9(3),\quad M_{15}(2),
\]
\[
Z_3(5),\quad Z_5(3),\quad Z_9(2),
\]
and corresponding upper-right-corner bounds.

The exact mismatch is decisive:

- CHHN proves **four generators in dimension five**, \(M_5(4)\), not four in dimension four;
- CHHN proves **three generators in dimension five**, \(Z_5(3)\), not three in dimension four.

CHHN’s Proposition 4 gives the general rank-one reduction \(Z_d(k)\le M_d(k+1)\). Its Theorems 4 and 5 give a cyclic block/phase trade that maps \(hk+1\) generators in dimension \(d\) to \(h+1\) generators in dimension \(kd\). Applied to five \(3\times3\) generators with \(h=k=2\), the literal packing has three \(6\times6\) generators. Theorem 7 exploits a distinguished common coordinate and reduces the corresponding structured zero problem to three generators in dimension

\[
1+k(d-1)=1+2(3-1)=5.
\]

Thus the exact closest CHHN result is the five-dimensional structured packing. No theorem in CHHN performs the proposed two-dimensional overlap quotient to reach four dimensions.

CHHN also contains a standard invariant-space reduction: the effective state dimension of a scalar representation can be reduced to the span generated by reachable rows/columns. That is related to quotient/restriction compression, but not to the proposed anti-diagonal identification of two copies along a common word plane.

**Verdict:** exact prior art for the main neighboring tools; no exact numerical or mechanism match.

### 4.5 Neary 2013 versus the refereed 2015 paper

Turlough Neary, “Undecidability in Binary Tag Systems and the Post Correspondence Problem for Four Pairs of Words,” arXiv:1312.6700v1, 19 December 2013, claimed four-pair PCP and consequently mortality for five \(3\times3\) matrices and two \(15\times15\) matrices.

Stable link: https://arxiv.org/abs/1312.6700

Version history: one arXiv version only. It was not formally withdrawn, but its central four-pair statement was not retained in the refereed successor.

The refereed successor is:

Turlough Neary, “Undecidability in Binary Tag Systems and the Post Correspondence Problem for Five Pairs of Words,” *STACS 2015*, LIPIcs 30, pp. 649–661.

Stable link: https://doi.org/10.4230/LIPIcs.STACS.2015.649

The STACS paper proves five-pair PCP and derives six \(3\times3\) mortality generators. It explicitly leaves the smaller PCP cases open. Rote later identifies a defect in the terminal-pair argument and discusses a repair.

The 2013 preprint matters for priority only for the numerical claim \(M_3(5)\). It does not claim \(M_4(4)\), \(Z_4(3)\), a two-plane quotient, a suffix-parity decoder, an all-placement Hankel certificate, or the phase-fusion obstruction.

**Verdict:** not an exact anticipation of any proposed claim here; historically relevant to how unrefereed numerical announcements should be described.

### 4.6 Nicolas’s bounded GPCP baseline

François Nicolas, “(Generalized) Post Correspondence Problem and semi-Thue systems,” arXiv:0802.0726v5.

Version history:

- v1: 6 February 2008;
- v2: 10 February 2008;
- v3: 13 February 2008;
- v4: 14 February 2008;
- v5: 12 November 2008.

Stable link: https://arxiv.org/abs/0802.0726

Nicolas defines bounded GPCP using a source alphabet of size \(k\) plus fixed boundary words, and proves reductions yielding undecidability at \(GPCP(5)\). The notes record \(GPCP(2)\) decidable and \(GPCP(3),GPCP(4)\) open at that time. Because the witness is in the free monoid, the empty-word convention must be checked separately when translating to \(X^+\).

**Verdict:** exact background for generator counting and boundary conventions; not prior art for the proposed compression.

### 4.7 Rote 2024/2025

Günter Rote, “Probabilistic Finite Automaton Emptiness Is Undecidable for a Fixed Automaton,” arXiv:2412.05198v1 (6 December 2024), full version of the peer-reviewed MFCS 2025 paper.

Stable links:

- https://arxiv.org/abs/2412.05198
- https://doi.org/10.4230/LIPIcs.MFCS.2025.86

Status: one arXiv version; refereed proceedings article in LIPIcs 345, Article 86, published in 2025.

Rote is the closest post-CHHN source in two respects.

First, it diagnoses the special final role in Neary’s construction and operationally merges a forced pair into a boundary vector. This is related to endpoint absorption, but it does not state the proposed bounded-GPCP or \(4\times4\) mortality theorem.

Second, Rote gives a binary alphabet reduction for matrices sharing a distinguished first row. A prefix-free code assigns binary codewords to the old letters, and every arbitrary binary word is interpreted by decoding its longest complete prefix; an absorbing coordinate controls the residue. The resulting dimension formula is \(1+(k-1)(d-1)\) in the stated setting.

This is strong related prior art for phase control, a total arbitrary-word decoder, and saving a distinguished coordinate. It differs from the proposal in all material details: first row rather than first column, prefix coding rather than suffix parity, a probabilistic/rational weighted-automaton setting rather than the stated integer mortality theorem, no anti-diagonal quotient, and no \(Z_4(3)\) or \(M_4(4)\) conclusion.

**Verdict:** related prior art for control-word decoding and distinguished-coordinate compression; not exact prior art.

Paul C. Bell, Igor Potapov, and Pavel Semukhin, “On the Mortality Problem: From Multiplicative Matrix Equations to Linear Recurrence Sequences and Beyond,” appeared in MFCS 2019 and as *Information and Computation* 281 (2021), Article 104736.

Stable links:

- https://arxiv.org/abs/1902.10188
- https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ic.2021.104736

## 5. Prior art for the proposed compression mechanism

### 5.1 PCP/GPCP matrix encodings

The broad matrix-encoding lineage is old:

- Paterson encodes PCP into \(3\times3\) integer mortality.
- Claus-type constructions enforce endpoint roles.
- HHH sharpen generator counts using endpoint structure.
- CHHN uses a base-value upper-triangular morphism and fixed boundary vectors.
- Igor Potapov, “From Post Systems to the Reachability Problems for Matrix Semigroups and Multicounter Automata,” DLT 2004, reduces \(PCP(n)\) to vector reachability for \(n\) \(4\times4\) integer matrices, yielding seven-generator undecidability.

Stable link for Potapov: https://doi.org/10.1007/978-3-540-30550-7_29

Potapov’s target is vector reachability, and the count is seven; it is a convention-matched integer \(4\times4\) source but not a three-generator scalar-zero theorem.

CHHN’s common-column family is the closest exact structural ancestor. The common coordinate is used to save states in a cyclic packing. No source located in this lineage states that two semantic roles agree on a common two-dimensional invariant word plane after an integral unimodular basis change.

### 5.2 Alphabet reduction and phase/toggle encodings

Relevant sources include:

- Julien Cassaigne and Juhani Karhumäki, “Examples of Undecidable Problems for 2-Generator Matrix Semigroups,” *Theoretical Computer Science* 204(1–2):29–34 (1998). Stable bibliographic link: https://dblp.org/rec/journals/tcs/CassaigneK98
- Vesa Halava and Mika Hirvensalo, “Improved Matrix Pair Undecidability Results,” *Acta Informatica* 44(3–4):191–205 (2007). Stable link: https://doi.org/10.1007/s00236-007-0047-y
- CHHN 2014, Theorems 4, 5, and 7.
- Rote 2025, binary prefix coding with arbitrary-word residue handling.

These sources establish that control alphabets can be traded for block dimensions and that malformed or incomplete codewords require a global converse argument. CHHN’s block matrices cyclically move payloads through phase slots; Rote’s construction uses a prefix code and an absorbing state. Neither source uses the quotient

\[
(V\oplus V)\big/\{(p,-p):p\in E\}
\]

or a suffix-parity transducer mapping three control letters surjectively onto four semantic roles.

**Verdict:** broad mechanism family is established; the proposed paired-role realization is not located.

### 5.3 Quotients, invariant subspaces, and amalgamation

As an abstract linear-algebra construction, identifying two copies of a common subspace is standard. The pushout of embeddings \(E\hookrightarrow V\) and \(E\hookrightarrow V\) can be represented as

\[
(V\oplus V)/\{(p,-p):p\in E\}.
\]

For finite-dimensional spaces, the dimension \(2\dim V-\dim E\) is the standard quotient dimension. This generic fact has no meaningful novelty claim by itself.

In weighted-automaton and linear-system realization theory, reachable and observable subspaces are likewise standard sources of restriction and quotient minimization. CHHN includes a concrete invariant row-space compression lemma.

The bibliographic question is therefore not whether quotients or amalgamated sums existed, but whether they had been combined with:

1. paired semantic roles;
2. a phase-toggle representation;
3. a total suffix-parity decoder;
4. a shared two-dimensional word plane;
5. preservation of the scalar zero language; and
6. the exact \(3\)-generator, \(4\)-state integer conclusion.

No such combination was found.

### 5.4 Common-column restrictions

CHHN explicitly constructs a structured zero-reachability family with a distinguished common column and uses that structure in its improved dimension trade. Rote uses the row-oriented analogue in a different setting. Thus “all generators share a distinguished coordinate” is not novel.

The exact proposed restriction remains unmatched because the literature stops at dimension five for three zero-reachability generators. No located result proves undecidability for three \(4\times4\) integer matrices all having first column \(e_1\).

## 6. Rank-one separators and the mortality converse

### 6.1 Established separator algebra

The rank-one identity behind separator reductions is classical. If \(P=CL\), then

\[
P\,Y\,P=C\,(LYC)\,L.
\]

Consequently, a scalar zero can be turned into a zero matrix when sandwiched between copies of \(P\). HHH’s rank-one idempotent construction and CHHN Proposition 4 are direct prior art.

The exact convention match is strong:

- finite integer matrices;
- one additional generator;
- a nonempty product;
- scalar zero converted to full-matrix zero.

Therefore, any priority claim must not present “add one rank-one separator” as a new general reduction.

### 6.2 What was not found

The difficult direction is excluding spurious zero products in an arbitrary word over the active generators and \(P\). Existing arguments ensure that exterior vectors do not vanish by using endpoint structure, nonsingularity, or a specialized normal form.

No source was found using the hypothesis

\[
A_i e_1=e_1\quad\text{for every active generator }A_i
\]

as a direct replacement for nonsingularity in the arbitrary-product converse of the rank-one separator construction. This is the bibliographically distinct part of the proposed separator argument.

**Verdict:** the separator is exact prior art; the common-fixed-column converse is not.

## 7. The CHHN packing and the Hankel-rank-six obstruction

### 7.1 Classical theorem

For a scalar series \(s:\Sigma^*\to K\) over a field \(K\), define its Hankel matrix by

\[
H_s(u,v)=s(uv),\qquad u,v\in\Sigma^*.
\]

A linear representation of dimension \(n\) has the form

\[
s(w)=\lambda\,\mu(w)\,\gamma
\]

with \(n\times n\) letter matrices. The classical realization theorem identifies the minimum possible exact representation dimension with \(\operatorname{rank} H_s\).

Principal sources and standard references are:

- M. P. Schützenberger, “On the Definition of a Family of Automata,” *Information and Control* 4 (1961), 245–270.
- J. W. Carlyle and A. Paz, “Realizations by Stochastic Finite Automata,” *Journal of Computer and System Sciences* 5(1):26–40 (1971). Stable link: https://doi.org/10.1016/S0022-0000(71)80005-3
- Michel Fliess, “Matrices de Hankel,” *Journal de Mathématiques Pures et Appliquées* 53 (1974), 197–222; erratum 54 (1975), 481.
- Arto Salomaa and Matti Soittola, *Automata-Theoretic Aspects of Formal Power Series*, Springer, 1978.
- Jean Berstel and Christophe Reutenauer, *Noncommutative Rational Series with Applications*, Cambridge University Press, 2010. Stable link: https://doi.org/10.1017/CBO9780511760860
- Manfred Droste, Werner Kuich, and Heiko Vogler, eds., *Handbook of Weighted Automata*, Springer, 2009. Stable link: https://doi.org/10.1007/978-3-642-01492-5

Accordingly, a displayed \(6\times6\) submatrix with nonzero determinant is a standard certificate that the exact Hankel rank is at least six. If the series already has a six-dimensional representation, the same certificate proves exact rank six and rules out every exact representation of dimension at most five.

This theorem is over a field. An integer coefficient series may be viewed over \(\mathbb Q\), so the field-valued lower bound is the relevant one for excluding integer or rational five-state representations.

### 7.2 No CHHN-specific certificate found

The search covered combinations of:

- “CHHN” or the full author list;
- \(6\times6\) packing, cyclic/block packing, generator-dimension trade;
- Hankel matrix, Hankel rank, linear representation rank;
- weighted automaton minimization;
- separator placement, punctuation placement, all placements;
- exact five-state realization.

No paper, preprint, thesis, report, or proceedings contribution was found that computes the Hankel rank of the scalar coefficient series arising from the standard CHHN \(M_3(5)\to M_6(3)\) packing. No all-placement theorem was found.

Rote’s discussion of small weighted/probabilistic automata is adjacent, and it reports compact constructions, but it does not provide this rank lower bound.

### 7.3 Exact-series versus zero-language scope

The weighted-automata theorem concerns equality of the whole coefficient series:

\[
s(w)=s'(w)\quad\text{for every word }w.
\]

It does not by itself exclude a different low-dimensional series \(t\) satisfying only

\[
t(w)=0\iff s(w)=0.
\]

This distinction is standard in weighted automata: minimization/equivalence of series is different from recognizing the support or zero set. The proposed obstruction is therefore accurately scoped only as an obstruction to **exact** five-state realization.

**Verdict:** exact prior art for the abstract lower-bound theorem; no prior art found for the CHHN-specific certificate.

## 8. The phase-fusion obstruction and regular positional-value languages

### 8.1 No forcing theorem found

No source was found for a theorem asserting that any one-state phase fusion, one-dimensional overlap, or rank-one punctuation scheme must place the punctuation action on a one-sided word-value plane. Exact phrase searches and broader searches under “phase encoding,” “toggle encoding,” “punctuation matrix,” “overlap quotient,” “amalgamated representation,” “one-sided word plane,” and “positional-value matrix language” produced no match.

The closest literature concerns:

- block/cyclic phase encodings in CHHN;
- binary alphabet reduction in Cassaigne–Karhumäki and Halava–Hirvensalo;
- Rote’s prefix-code decoder and absorbing residue state;
- standard quotient and invariant-subspace realization theory.

None proves a necessity or impossibility result of the proposed form.

### 8.2 Effective regularity of affine digit constraints

The second half of the proposed obstruction has established antecedents. Fixed-base digit encodings of Presburger-definable sets are regular/effectively automata-recognizable. Relevant sources include:

- J. Richard Büchi, “Weak Second-Order Arithmetic and Finite Automata,” *Zeitschrift für mathematische Logik und Grundlagen der Mathematik* 6 (1960), 66–92.
- Véronique Bruyère, Georges Hansel, Christian Michaux, and Roger Villemaire, “Logic and \(p\)-Recognizable Sets of Integers,” *Bulletin of the Belgian Mathematical Society – Simon Stevin* 1(2):191–238 (1994). Stable link: https://doi.org/10.36045/bbms/1103408547
- Pierre Wolper and Bernard Boigelot, “An Automata-Theoretic Approach to Presburger Arithmetic Constraints,” SAS 1995, LNCS 983, pp. 21–32. Stable link: https://doi.org/10.1007/3-540-60360-3_30
- Bernard Boigelot, Stéphane Rassart, and Pierre Wolper, “On the Expressiveness of Real and Integer Arithmetic Automata,” ICALP 1998, LNCS 1443, pp. 152–163. Stable link: https://doi.org/10.1007/BFb0055049
- Felix Klaedtke, “Bounds on the Automata Size for Presburger Arithmetic,” *ACM Transactions on Computational Logic* 9(2), Article 11 (2008). Stable link: https://doi.org/10.1145/1342991.1342995
- Christiane Frougny, “Representations of Numbers and Finite Automata,” *Mathematical Systems Theory* 25:37–60 (1992).

These sources cover linear equations, inequations, Boolean combinations, projection, and quantification over integer vectors represented digitwise in a fixed base. They justify the statement “the zero language is effectively regular” only after the matrix argument has reduced the zero condition to a fixed affine/Presburger relation under a specified digit convention.

They do not prove:

- that phase fusion forces such a relation;
- that the relation is one-sided;
- that rank-one punctuation has no access to another nonregular component;
- or that every malformed control word falls inside the same digit encoding.

**Verdict:** exact prior art for the conditional regularity endpoint; no prior art found for the proposed forcing bridge.

## 9. False positives and convention mismatches

The following sources or topic clusters appeared close in search results but do not match the requested theorem.

| Source/topic | Why it is not exact prior art |
|---|---|
| Gaubert–Katz, “Reachability Problems for Products of Matrices in Semirings,” IJAC 2006 | Gives general reductions and max-plus/tropical results. The coefficient semiring is not the ordinary integer ring for the undecidability statement at issue. |
| Potapov, DLT 2004 | Seven \(4\times4\) integer generators and vector reachability, not three-generator scalar zero. |
| Bell–Potapov, “On Undecidability Bounds for Matrix Decision Problems,” TCS 2008 | Important reachability/identity bounds, but not \(M_4(4)\) or structured \(Z_4(3)\). |
| Bell–Potapov–Semukhin 2019/2021 | Bounded-language products \(A_1^{m_1}\cdots A_t^{m_t}\), not arbitrary words in a free semigroup. |
| Breuillard–Kocharyan 2024/2025 | Rational matrix groups and corner/membership questions; inverses and group structure change the problem. |
| Halava–Niskanen 2024 | Rational \(3\times3\) matrices with integrality tests and a special identity problem, not unrestricted integer mortality. |
| Heckman 2019 | Decidability for \(2\times2\) families with at most one invertible generator; a different dimension and direction. |
| Potapov–Semukhin on \(\mathrm{SL}(2,\mathbb Z)\) | Decidability in a group/unimodular stratum, not the proposed undecidability. |
| Nonnegative matrix/NFA mortality papers | Nonnegative coefficients make zero-product behavior combinatorial; these do not establish signed-integer mortality bounds. |
| Probabilistic finite automata | Stochastic/rational matrices, threshold or acceptance semantics, and often words including the empty word; useful for encodings but not convention-identical. |
| Quantum or matrix-exponential reachability | Continuous/unitary/exponential semigroups, not finitely generated integer matrix semigroups. |
| 2026 work on Zariski closures or finite irreducible rational matrix semigroups | Mentions matrix mortality but addresses algebraic closure or finite-semigroup size, not the fixed numerical undecidability cells. |
| Weighted-automata approximation/minimization | Establishes approximate or exact series compression in general; no CHHN all-placement certificate. |
| “Phase fusion” in signal/image processing | Terminological coincidence only. |

A particularly important false positive is the statement “four matrices suffice”: CHHN does prove four mortality generators, but in dimension **five**, not four.

## 11. Search methodology and coverage

The search used the following source classes:

- arXiv title/abstract/full-text and version histories;
- DBLP records and linked electronic editions;
- Crossref/DOI publisher pages;
- Dagstuhl DROPS proceedings records;
- Springer, Wiley, Elsevier, ACM, World Scientific, and Cambridge records;
- institutional repositories and author copies from Turku, Liège, Liverpool, Technion, ETH, and other universities;
- TUCS technical-report records;
- theses and survey bibliographies;
- the reference lists and forward-citation neighborhoods of Paterson, HHH, CHHN, Neary, Nicolas, Rote, and the main weighted-automata realization references;
- exact phrase and semantic searches through the cutoff date.

Query families included:

- `matrix mortality`, `M_4(4)`, `four 4x4 integer matrices`, `matrix mortality four generators dimension four`;
- `scalar zero reachability`, `scalar reachability 4x4 three matrices`, `Z_4(3)`, `zero in the corner`;
- `common first column`, `common fixed column`, `e1 column matrix semigroup`;
- `PCP matrix encoding`, `GPCP matrix encoding`, `Claus instance`, `terminal tile`, `rank-one separator`, `outer product mortality`;
- `phase toggle`, `cyclic block matrix encoding`, `alphabet reduction matrix semigroup`, `suffix parity decoder`;
- `invariant subspace compression`, `quotient representation`, `amalgamated representation`, `pushout representation`;
- `CHHN 6x6 packing`, `Hankel rank matrix mortality`, `weighted automaton five states`, `all placement separator`;
- `phase fusion obstruction`, `one-sided word plane`, `positional value zero language`, `affine digit language regular`.

Exact proposed phrases—“paired-role compression,” “suffix parity” together with matrix mortality, “phase-fusion obstruction,” and “all-placement Hankel-rank-six”—returned no scholarly match.

Coverage limitations:

1. “No prior art found” is limited to publicly discoverable material through the cutoff.
2. Some subscription articles were checked through publisher abstracts, bibliographic metadata, author copies, and citing sources rather than publisher-hosted full text.
3. No claim is made about unpublished manuscripts, personal correspondence, or non-indexed local proceedings.
4. Generic algebraic facts were separated from application-specific priority to avoid both overclaiming novelty and treating a standard quotient as an anticipation of the theorem.

## 12. Recommended priority wording

A bibliographically defensible statement is:

> A search of the public literature through 22 July 2026 found no prior proof of undecidability for \(M_4(4)\), nor of scalar zero reachability for three \(4\times4\) integer matrices sharing first column \(e_1\). CHHN’s closest established bounds are \(M_5(4)\) and structured \(Z_5(3)\). The general rank-one scalar-to-mortality separator and the Hankel-rank characterization of exact weighted-automaton state complexity are prior art; no prior source was found for the proposed common-column converse, paired-role anti-diagonal quotient with suffix-parity decoding, CHHN-specific all-placement rank-six certificate, or phase-fusion forcing theorem. Effective regularity of the final affine positional-value language is classical once the required Presburger form has been established.

Avoid an unqualified “first ever” statement unless author inquiries and any relevant non-indexed proceedings searches are also completed. Neary’s 2013 v1 should be disclosed when discussing the separate \(M_3(5)\) history, but it is not an anticipation of \(M_4(4)\) or of the proposed four-dimensional mechanism.
