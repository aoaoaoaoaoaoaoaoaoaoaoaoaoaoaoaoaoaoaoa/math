01 · The strange question

Can multiplication eventually kill everything?

A finite list of matrices looks harmless. Yet the set of all products made from that list is an infinite, branching object. Matrix mortality asks whether one branch ever reaches the zero matrix.

Matrix mortality Md(k)

Input: at most k integer d × d matrices A1, …, Ak. Question: does some nonempty product

Ai₁Ai₂ ··· Aiₙ = 0

exist, with arbitrary length n, arbitrary order, and repetition allowed?

A “yes” answer is recognizable: enumerate products by length and stop when zero appears. The difficulty is a “no.” Failure through the first trillion products says nothing about the next one. Undecidability means there is no algorithm that always settles both cases.

The product is a word

Choosing matrices in sequence is choosing letters from a finite alphabet. Multiplication gives the word a semantics.

Length is unbounded

The matrices are fixed, but the witness may be arbitrarily long. That is where an entire computation can hide.

Order matters

Matrix multiplication is noncommutative. The semigroup retains the sequential structure needed to simulate a machine.

Integers carry exact state

No numerical approximation is involved. Large entries act as exact encodings of concatenated strings.

This is the peculiar charm of the problem: no elaborate machine remains visible at the end. Only a handful of tiny square arrays and the question “can they multiply to zero?”

02 · The undecidability staircase

How few, and how small?

Unrestricted mortality for 3 × 3 integer matrices has been known undecidable since Paterson. The finer game fixes both resources: dimension d and number of generators k. A result at one point propagates upward by allowing more generators or padding matrices into larger dimensions, so the meaningful record is a descending staircase of minimal known undecidable points.

The previous minimal known undecidability antichain, due to Cassaigne, Halava, Harju, and Nicolas, was

M3(6),   M5(4),   M9(3),   M15(2).

A 2013 technical report had announced M3(5), but its four-pair PCP premise was not retained in the refereed successor; the accepted literature continued to list M3(6). The historical distinction is recorded below.

RegimeBeforeAfter
Smallest dimensionM3(6)M3(5)
Four generatorsM5(4)M5(4)
Three generatorsM9(3)M6(3)
Two generatorsM15(2)M12(2)

The first move, from six matrices to five in dimension three, is the result proved here. The other improvements follow from an established generator-to-dimension packing theorem:

Md(hk + 1) ≤m Mkd(h + 1).

Here ≤m means a computable translation of instances that preserves yes and no answers. Substituting 5 = 2·2 + 1 and 5 = 1·4 + 1 turns M3(5) into M6(3) and M12(2). One saved generator at the source moves three separate steps of the known-undecidability staircase. This is not a complete decidability frontier: the cells immediately below remain open.

03 · Words compute

The route through correspondence

The reduction does not compile a Turing machine straight into matrices. It passes through two austere models of computation where concatenation is the only moving part.

Tag systems: computation as a queue

A tag system holds a finite word. At each step it reads the first symbol, appends a prescribed word, then deletes a fixed number β of symbols from the front. It halts when fewer than β symbols remain. The rule is brutally local, but carefully chosen binary tag systems are universal.

Post correspondence: computation as equality

A Post correspondence instance gives pairs of words (ui, vi). It asks for an index word i1···in such that the upper and lower concatenations agree:

ui₁···uiₙ = vi₁···viₙ.

For example, the pairs (a, ab) and (ba, a) solve themselves in that order, because a·ba = ab·a.

Generalized PCP (GPCP) allows fixed words at the boundaries. That slight change is decisive here: a tile once treated as a variable instruction can instead become fixed punctuation.

The terminal-tile snag

Neary produced five PCP pairs from an undecidable restricted tag system, with the fifth intended to act only as a terminator. The published terminal-pair argument contained an error later diagnosed by Rote, who proposed a longer unary guard. For mortality there is a cleaner route: make terminal behavior irrelevant.

The cure is not to defend the old terminal tile. Remove it from the moving alphabet and prove that the first four pairs already recognize halting when supplied with a fixed right boundary.

This is the conceptual step that saves a matrix. The matrix algebra afterward is exact but standard in spirit.

04 · The source mechanism

Four tiles force lawful histories

Fix β ≥ 3 and a word q over b,c. Consider the tag system

b ↦ b,   c ↦ qb,   I = dropβ−1(q)b.

Neary’s universal compiler produces systems of this form. Encode queue letters into binary blocks by

H(b) = 1 0β 1,   H(c) = 1,   M = 1 0β.

Extend H by concatenation and introduce four word pairs:

TileUpper wordLower wordRole
Rc11 H(q) 1 0apply the c-rule
Rb1 0β 11 1 0apply the b-rule
Dc10erase one c
Db1 0β 10erase one b

For a tile word w, let U(w) and V(w) be its upper and lower concatenations. The source theorem is the exact equivalence

Four-tile tag compiler
∃w:   U(w)M = V(w)   ⇔   the tag system halts from I.

It holds when β−1 divides |q| and |q| ≥ β−1. These arithmetic hypotheses describe a wider class than Neary’s compiler outputs; the outputs form an undecidable subfamily inside it.

Neary’s compiler can be padded computably so that these arithmetic hypotheses hold. Thus only his tag-universality lemma is imported; no terminal-pair argument enters the proof.

Technical seam: why Neary’s compiler satisfies the arithmetic hypotheses

Neary has β = 10p and |qb| = βs. If n is the input length and r the maximum cyclic appendant length, take

B = max{11(n+p)+β−2, 11p, 11r, 1},   x = ⌈(B−1)/(β−1)⌉,   s = x(β−1)+1.

This computable choice lies in Neary’s permitted padding range and makes every exponent in his Table 2 nonnegative. It gives

|q| = β[x(β−1)+1]−1 = (xβ+1)(β−1),

so β−1 divides |q| and |q| ≥ β−1.

The soundness problem

The difficult implication must begin with an arbitrary tile word satisfying the equality, not with a word already assumed to describe the intended simulation. The proof has three locks.

Zero runs force blocks

The upper side U(w)M is an H-encoded word followed by M. Every positive run of zeros therefore has length exactly β. On the lower side, a rule tile ends with one zero; an erase tile contributes one more; the next rule begins with a one.

The payload H(q) contributes only complete β-runs and returns the scan to zero gap before the rule tile’s final 10; it cannot conceal a tile boundary. Equality therefore leaves no freedom: every rule tile must be followed by exactly β−1 erase tiles. Thus any matching word factors into blocks

Ra₀ Da₁ ··· Daβ−1,

one block for each length-β “stroke” a0···aβ−1 consumed from a queue. This finite-state scan is exhaustive; malformed but prefix-compatible choices do not escape it.

The global equality becomes a queue certificate

Decode the forced blocks. Let C(S) be all strokes concatenated, and let P(S) be all rule appendants selected by their first letters. Cancelling the initial block from the terminal equality yields

C(S)b = IP(S).

This one equation records an entire alleged queue history.

Prefix cancellation recovers lawful execution

If the current queue I is long enough, both it and the first stroke are prefixes of the same certified word. The stroke has length β, so it must equal the first β queue symbols. Delete it, append the rule word selected by its head, cancel the common prefix, and repeat.

If a short queue appears early, stop. Nothing after a halt needs interpretation. Hence every global certificate contains a genuine terminating tag execution, even when its unused suffix is nonsense.

Reusable source lemma

The cancellation step is not peculiar to Neary. For any deletion-width-β tag system, let S be a list of β-letter strokes, C(S) their concatenation, and P(S) the concatenation of the appendants selected by their heads. If

C(S)F = IP(S)   and   |F| < β,

then the tag system halts from I. The proof is the prefix-cancellation induction above. To reuse the source method elsewhere, one need only engineer a word code whose equality (i) forces candidate labels into stroke blocks and (ii) decodes into this certificate. The semantic soundness lemma then applies unchanged; its formal statement is tagHaltsFrom_of_history.

The reverse implication records the β symbols deleted at each real tag step. Every reachable queue ends in b, and its length is always 1 modulo β−1. A queue shorter than β must therefore be the one-letter queue b. Reversing the cancellation constructs the required tile word.

A clean five-pair PCP repair

The matrix theorem already follows from the fixed boundary M, but ordinary PCP can also be repaired without a long unary guard. Add a fresh symbol # and a fifth pair

(M#, #).

At the first occurrence of #, prefix compatibility of U(w)M# and V(w)# forces U(w)M = V(w) exactly. That first occurrence already closes a solution prefix. Call a PCP solution primitive when it is not a concatenation of two shorter nonempty solutions; any material after the first marked prefix would make the whole solution nonprimitive. A fixed-length code, 0↦00, 1↦01, #↦11, returns the construction to a binary alphabet without changing prefix comparability. Every primitive solution uses the fifth pair exactly once and last.

This marker step is generic: if the ordinary pairs have no solution by themselves, a fixed-right-boundary equation U(w)s = V(w)t becomes ordinary PCP after adding (s#, t#), and every primitive solution uses the marked pair exactly once and last.

05 · The matrix compiler

Turn equality into annihilation

What remains is to make concatenation happen inside 3 × 3 multiplication and to amplify one zero entry into the zero matrix.

First recode binary symbols as nonzero base-three digits, 0↦1 and 1↦2. For a digit word x, let σ(x) be its base-three value. Nonzero digits prevent leading-zero ambiguity, so σ is injective across words of every length.

Ψ(x,y) =
1σ(y)σ(x)−σ(y) 03|y|3|x|−3|y| 003|x|

The entries have been placed so that ordinary matrix multiplication performs word concatenation:

Concatenation
Ψ(xx′,yy′) = Ψ(x,y)Ψ(x′,y′)
Equality test
e1TΨ(x,y)e3 = 0 ⇔ x = y
Nonsingularity
det Ψ(x,y) = 3|x|+|y| ≠ 0

Let X1, …, X4 encode the four ordinary word pairs. Write ε for the empty word, encode the fixed boundary by X★ = Ψ(M, ε), and set

c = X★e3,   A★ = c e1T = X★e3e1T.

The five mortality generators are X1, …, X4, A★. The first four are nonsingular and upper triangular. The last is a nonzero outer product, hence rank one.

A matching word gives a zero product

If w satisfies U(w)M = V(w), the corresponding matrix product Xw satisfies

A★XwA★ = c(e1TXwX★e3)e1T = 0.

The boundary matrix plays both bookends. This is where the sixth generator disappears.

The arbitrary-product converse

A reduction is not sound until every arbitrary product has been classified. A product without A★ is a product of nonsingular matrices, so it is not zero. Any product with t copies of A★ has a unique form

P0A★P1A★ ··· A★Pt,

where every Pj is an ordinary product. Expanding the rank-one factors collapses the whole matrix word to

(P0c) [∏1≤j<t e1TPjc] (e1TPt).

The exterior column and row are nonzero because the ordinary blocks are nonsingular. With one separator the outer product is nonzero. With two or more, the product vanishes only if an internal scalar vanishes. But each such scalar is exactly the ternary test for some equation U(w)M = V(w).

Reusable fixed-boundary compiler

Let α be any finite source alphabet, let u, v: α → {0,1}*, and fix right boundaries s, t. Put Xa = Ψ(u(a), v(a)), X★ = Ψ(s, t), and A★ = X★e3e1T. Then

{Xa : a∈α} ∪ {A★} is mortal   ⇔   ∃w∈α*: u(w)s = v(w)t.

The compiler costs one rank-one generator beyond |α| and needs no prescribed product shape: the fracture formula classifies every word automatically. Its exact machine-checked form is absorbedFamilyInt_mortal_iff_terminal_match. Endpoint absorption has antecedents; the useful contribution here is its interface with the new four-letter source.

Main result M3(5) is undecidable.

It remains undecidable with four nonsingular upper triangular integer generators and one nonzero integer generator of rational rank one.

06 · Status and verification

What is proved, and what is trusted?

The proof has a precise trust boundary. Lean checks every new instance-level implication, from arbitrary tile words through arbitrary five-matrix products. The final undecidability conclusion imports Neary’s established universality theorem.

Machine-checked

The four-tile synchronization theorem, queue-history soundness, fresh-marker PCP repair, ternary representation, rank-one compiler, and converse for every five-matrix word.

Standard logical base

Lean reports only the usual mathlib foundations: propositional extensionality, classical choice, and quotient soundness. Every generated proof term is checked by Lean’s kernel.

External theorem

Neary’s peer-reviewed Lemma 9 supplies undecidability of the restricted tag family. Its universality compiler has not yet been reconstructed in Lean.

Independent falsification

A separate executable search scoured 20,272,272 arbitrary tile words without finding a mismatch between terminal equations and reconstructed halting histories. This is a bug hunt, not part of the proof.

Accordingly, the new instance-level bridge is machine-checked. The full sentence “M3(5) is undecidable” combines that bridge with an accepted external universality theorem, exactly as an ordinary paper imports earlier results. A completely closed formal proof would additionally formalize Neary’s machine-to-tag compiler and the computability wrapper.

Historical note

Neary’s one-version 2013 technical report publicly claimed both four-pair PCP and M3(5). The claim was not retained in his refereed 2015 successor, which explicitly left four-pair PCP open, and later literature continued to report the six-matrix bound. No formal withdrawal or accepted independent proof is known.

The present argument takes a different route: it proves the fixed-boundary language of the four ordinary 2015 pairs directly, then applies the rank-one endpoint compiler. It is therefore not the first announcement of the number; it is a proof of the previously unestablished case and, to our knowledge, the first valid proof of binary-target four-generator GPCP. The novelty audit records the search and the deliberately narrow priority claim.
